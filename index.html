FAQ

What are the mystical origins of Heikki Lunta?
Heikki Lunta was a creation of David Riutta, in the 1970s with WMPL radio. The first song of Heikki Lunta was created to respond to a dearth of snow prior to the snowmobile race. The first song's great success led to so much snow that the snowmobile race was cancelled. The second song is an enchanting ballad of escape from the wrath of those who could not race.
Do more and more people believe in Heikki's message and dance?
The Heikki Lunta story has become universal in this age of globalization. Heikki's story reflects the true paganism that might yet again come, and that has already been, in northern Finland.
Is Heikki Lunta really that powerful?
The snow-making powers of Heikki Lunta are well documented.

I don't snowmobile. Can I still believe in Heikki Lunta?
Snowmobiling is not a prerequisite. I don't know how to snowmobile myself, and don't have an interest in learning. Here is the key: people that support Heikki Lunta typically share passions for snow, dance, Finland, and/or the Keweenaw Peninsula of Michigan. Many also have great reverence for Kalevalic heros.
Can I drink thimbleberry margaritas and drive a snowmobile?
No. It is not a good idea and it would be illegal to drive any motorized vehicle after drinking a moderate amount of thimbleberry margarita (please see relevant laws in vigor).
